Code reviews

Think of us as your past self

A lot of the time we don’t have the same context to rely on as you do.

You might need to explain things differently to us that you would a senior developer.

If you and I were watching the Olympics together and I told you 'she cheated her double axel and had a bad wrap’, you’d have no idea what I meant. What I could say to you though is; 'she… actually rotated her foot before taking off into the air (which is called cheating), and her left leg came up above her knee in the air (that’s called wrapping) which is really poor style’.

You might be fuzzy on what a double axel is, so you go Google that, then you follow up by trying to figure out what the heck ‘cheated’ and ‘wrap’ mean in this context, and before you know it what could be explained in one sentence, has turned into a 30 minute rabbit hole.

You might think this is a pain, but it will help you grow as you’ll have to think about a problem in a very different way.

Global State

Set data-state="something" on a slide and "something" will be added as a class to the document element when the slide is open. This lets you apply broader style changes, like switching the page background.

State Events

Additionally custom events can be triggered on a per slide basis by binding to the data-state name. 


Reveal.addEventListener( 'customevent', function() {
	console.log( '"customevent" has fired' );
} );
